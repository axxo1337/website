I created my channel while studying in [secondary school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_school) back in 2022. I never 
exactly knew what I wanted the channel to be aside from providing educational computer science content. I picked the name "aXXo" as I
have always been passionate about cybersecurity. It sounds like a play on the term "hacking". I also knew this was the 
pseudonym of an [ex-DVD ripper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AXXo), a legend of the early internet, and I chose it to honor him.

### Assembly tutorials

My very first videos consisted of a [course on the basics of x86_64 assembly programming](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL36bJ4YMRalhxtCw2trRtN4EV90S4cHj3).
It was nothing impressive really. At this time I had no editing skills, no organization and spoke very poor English. Even though 
I lacked in all these areas, I still figured out my branding the very first day:

![OG logo|center|200px|Original logo](/images/documents/youtube/og-logo.png)

This has been my logo for a while. The shape was supposed to represent an "A" as per the first letter of my channel's name.
I wanted dark background colors with white on the foreground. Later I chose to make the background even darker as I thought it 
looked professional.

The tutorials ended by covering logic gates and a few instructions I had not covered in earlier videos.

![Final series video thumbnail|center|auto|Thumbnail of the series' final video](/images/documents/youtube/final-assembly-series-video.png)

Two years later, in between many other videos, I did make a [new video](https://youtu.be/tTwlt6vJXmQ) covering almost everything this series went through, but with 
my newer branding and editing skills.

### Mascot

Following the assembly series I was left wondering what topic I would cover. With my past experience in game hacking, creating a series 
on this topic was the obvious choice, only I did not want to copy the likes of [GuidedHacking](https://guidedhacking.com/) and other pre-existing creators.
I noticed there were little to no videos explaining Java games using the [Java Native Interface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Java_Native_Interface) (JNI).
This was expected as very few games still relied on Java to this day. I would have stopped here if it were not for Minecraft.

This game sold over 350 million copies. Let's just say there was no reason to believe there would not be an audience for this subject. 
With this in mind I decided I would also need a little bit of rebranding. I felt my videos were too bland at this point and I figured
adding a mascot to add into the animations would improve that. And so was it born:

![Mascot over time|center|auto|History of my mascot](/images/documents/youtube/mascot.png)

The first iteration was slightly uncanny and I would animate it "stop-frame-style". It would take another year before I would understand 
how to make it look better and make it move like a real character.

#### Minecraft JNI series

Overall the series was a fair success. It significantly outperformed my assembly series and as of writing this gathered me 86'996 views 
and 552 subscribers. In comparison, the assembly series accumulated 8'165 views and 71 subscribers.

I ended the series out of a loss of interest and a desire to touch on other topics.
